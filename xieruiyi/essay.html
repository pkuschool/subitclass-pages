The pros and cons of solar energy

Solar energy has been widely used in various fields, it is one of the most important resources that will be able to lead we humans to a sustainable future. But does this resource have no drawbacks at all? Probably yes. Scientists and even engineers have questioned its trend since it was being used.

As a renewable energy, solar energy is very friendly to the environment. Non-renewable energy, such as gas, oil, and coal, greatly increase the amount of carbon dioxide emissions, and create a harmful effect to our atmosphere. In contrast, solar energy doesn't pollute the environment at all. In addition, as it is mostly collected through sun, we humans are able to use it without worrying we might run out of it, this way it decelerates the imbalance between human and nature. Furthermore, more studies and application have proved the effectiveness of solar energy. For instance, by 2017, the use of photovoltaic cells has produced more than 300 gigawatts of power worldwide.

Though solar energy has many advantages, many environmentalists have doubts and worries in its stableness. Because it collects energy from the sun, when there isn't so many sunshine, we won't have enough power to preserve our life aquirement. This means it is an unstable energy, and weather greatly limit its use. Despite weather, the solar energy collecting technique cannot save the energy it collects. Nowadays, when there is lack of sunshine, we mostly depend on traditional energy to give us electricity.

In conclusion, solar energy has a positive impact on our environment. We should continue to improve our techniques to reach a higher stableness on solar energy. With the widespread use of solar energy and a higher stableness on its technique, we can look forward to a more sustainable future.
